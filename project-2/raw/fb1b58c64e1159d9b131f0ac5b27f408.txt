        <section class="hl__show-more-text js-show-more-text"><div  class="hl__show-more-text__content js-show-more-text-content" id="showMoreText5831"><section class="hl__rich-text"><h3>Spaces</h3><h4>Reading Room</h4><p>The Fine Arts Library Reading Room is an open, light-filled space located on the main level of the library. <a href="https://library.harvard.edu/spaces?library=216&amp;room=361">Learn more about the Reading Room</a>.</p><h4>Study Carrels</h4><p>The Fine Arts Library has a limited number of carrels located in the book stacks.&nbsp; <a href="https://library.harvard.edu/spaces?library=216&amp;room=371&amp;room_type=1146">Learn more about applying for a study carrel</a>.</p><h4>Special Collections Study Room</h4><figure class="hl__figure--right hl__figure"><img loading="lazy" src="/sites/default/files/styles/content_embed/public/IMG_4413.jpg?itok=nFSL20U7" width="450" height="306" alt="The Fine Arts Library Special Collections Study Room, with chairs around a table" class="hl__image"><figcaption class="hl__figure__figcaption"><div class="hl__figure__text-caption">
                The Fine Arts Library Special Collections Study Room.

                          </div></figcaption></figure><p>The Fine Arts Library welcomes researchers to our Special Collections Study Room to view historical photographs, rare books, manuscript facsimiles, auction sales catalogs, archival collections, and other materials from our Special Collections, which do not circulate outside of the library.</p><h5>Access</h5><ul><li>The Special Collections Study Room is currently open by appointment to all researchers (both HUID holders and visiting researchers).</li><li>Note that non-HUID holders will need to register for an account with the <a href="https://library.harvard.edu/visitor-access#access-cards">Harvard Library Access and Borrowing Office</a> prior to accessing the library.</li></ul><h5>Accessibility of The Fine Arts Library&nbsp;</h5><ul><li>The back entrance to the Littauer Center, adjacent to the Littauer parking lot, is accessible by automatic door and ramp. There’s an elevator on Level-G, and the Fine Arts Library is located on Level-1.&nbsp;</li><li>There is an all-gender restroom on Level-1 next to the elevator in the Littauer Center, and accessible bathrooms are located on the Level-G.</li></ul><p>If you need assistance accessing the stacks or have other questions about accessibility, contact Fine Arts Library Access Services, <a href="mailto:falibcir@fas.harvard.edu">falibcir@fas.harvard.edu</a>.</p><h5>Schedule a Study Room Appointment</h5><ul><li>For the health and safety of our researchers and staff, we ask that you schedule an appointment to visit the Special Collections Study Room using the <a href="https://libcal.library.harvard.edu/space/114891">Appointment Request form</a>.</li><li>Appointments must be made at least 24 hours in advance.</li><li>No walk-in or same day appointments will be available.</li><li>Be sure to also request materials via the instructions below.</li></ul><h5>Request Materials to View</h5><ul><li>Special collections materials can be requested for viewing using the “Request to Scan or Visit” link in the HOLLIS record to connect to <a href="https://library.harvard.edu/services-tools/hollis-special-request">HOLLIS Special Request</a>.</li><li>Some requests, especially for materials held offsite, may require two to four business days to process.</li><li>Please note that HOLLIS Special Request does not send automated email confirmations. Feel free to <a href="mailto:falibref@fas.harvard.edu">contact us</a> to confirm availability ahead of scheduling your visit.</li><li>Be sure to also schedule a Study Room appointment via the instructions above.</li></ul><h5>&nbsp;</h5><h5>Request Materials to Digitize</h5><ul><li>Special collections materials can be requested for digitization using the “Request to Scan or Visit” link in the HOLLIS record to connect to <a href="https://library.harvard.edu/services-tools/hollis-special-request">HOLLIS Special Request</a>.</li><li>For course use or reference questions, <a href="mailto:falibref@fas.harvard.edu">contact us</a> directly.</li></ul><h5>Class Visits</h5><ul><li>The library may be able to accommodate small classes and sections in the Study Room for hands-on engagement with Special Collections materials.</li><li>Instructors may make a request via the <a href="https://classrequest.library.harvard.edu/">Special Collections and Archives Class Request Tool</a> or <a href="mailto:falibref@fas.harvard.edu">contact us</a> directly.</li><li>Virtual course sessions can be arranged for large or small classes as needed.</li></ul><h5>General Guidelines</h5><p>Here are some things to keep in mind for your visit:</p><ul><li><a href="https://learn.library.harvard.edu/handle">Handling guidelines</a> must be conscientiously followed in order to preserve library materials for future use. Library staff are always available to provide guidance.</li><li>Researchers should not remove any items from the Study Room or rearrange any items within a folder or any folders within a box. If material appears to be out of order, please notify library staff.</li><li>If you need help in handling Special Collections materials, or if you have any questions about them, please don't hesitate to ask the library staff, who are always willing to provide assistance and suggestions to assist your research.</li><li>Researchers are welcome to bring pencils, laptops, tablets, phones (in silent mode), digital cameras, and other materials to assist in their research.</li><li>Researchers may bring personal books or papers into the Reading Room only if these items are necessary for their immediate research.</li><li>All other materials and personal property not essential for research -- including coats, bags, backpacks, computer cases, and other belongings -- must be stored in the lockers provided.</li><li>We offer a number of supplies that may help you in your research, including: pencils, note taking paper, magnifying glasses, tape measures and rulers, a small light box, and headphones.</li><li>The library does not permit the use of pens, tripods, scanners, camera flashes, special lighting, or any other equipment that rests directly on collection material or may pose a risk of damage.</li><li>Researchers are welcome to photograph most materials. Some collections may be subject to donor stipulations that limit or prohibit photography. Researchers are asked not to take pictures of the Study Room itself, other patrons, or library staff.</li><li>In order to preserve relative quiet for our researchers, we ask laptop computer and digital camera users to turn off all audible features, if possible.</li></ul></section></div><div class="hl__show-more-text__controls" aria-hidden="true"><a class="hl__link-tag hl__link-tag--with-arrow js-show-more-text-link" href="#" title="" aria-expanded="false" aria-controls="showMoreText5831"><span class="hl__link-tag__toggle-text">Show Less</span><span>Show More</span></a></div></section>  </div>

      <div class="hl__full-template__page-content hl__full-template__page-content--stacked" id="about" tabindex="-1"><div class="hl__comp-header" tab-index="-1"><h2 class="hl__comp-heading ">  About

We are dedicated to providing meaningful access to library resources through active curation and long-term stewardship of diverse collections, as well as specialized staff expertise and accessible user-centered services. The library strives to uphold its values of artistic and intellectual freedom, diversity, and anti-racism, and be a site of connection and inclusion for our community, both near and far.&nbsp;</p><h3>History</h3><p>The Fine Arts Library is the primary resource for the study of the history of art and architecture and related subjects at Harvard University.</p><figure class="hl__figure--right hl__figure"><img loading="lazy" src="/sites/default/files/styles/content_embed/public/02_Fogg%20Art%20Museum_Interior_1925-27.jpg?itok=OzqhaHpR" width="450" height="303" alt="Fogg Art Museum interior circa 1925" class="hl__image"><figcaption class="hl__figure__figcaption"><div class="hl__figure__text-caption">

            </div></figcaption></figure><p>Since the founding of the Fogg Art Museum in 1895, the library has served the needs of teaching faculty, art museum staff, undergraduate and graduate students, researchers, and historians.</p><p>The history of the library is intertwined with Harvard's role in the development of academic programs in art and architectural history and museum studies. With the 1874 appointment of Charles Eliot Norton, Harvard became the first university in the United States to institute a professorship in art history.<br>
&nbsp;</p><p>In 1927, Harvard dedicated a new Fogg Museum building, which was designed to display art in a setting together with classrooms, conservation labs, painting studios, and a research library.</p><p>In 1962, Widener Library’s arts-related holdings were transferred to an expanded library space within the Fogg Museum. At this time, the Fogg Museum Library became a part of the Harvard College Library and was given its current name, the Fine Arts Library.</p><figure class="hl__figure--right hl__figure"><img loading="lazy" src="/sites/default/files/styles/content_embed/public/01_Fine%20Arts%20Library_Reading%20Room_1951.jpg?itok=vCIkjK2w" width="450" height="365" alt="The Fine Arts Library reading room circa 1951, with students studying at tables" class="hl__image"><figcaption class="hl__figure__figcaption"><div class="hl__figure__text-caption">

            </div></figcaption></figure><p>The newly combined collection formed one of the largest art research collections in the United States, serving as a model for other institutions for building their art libraries.</p><p>Other notable milestones for the Fine Arts Library include:</p><ul><li><strong>1978</strong>: The <a href="https://library.harvard.edu/collections/rubel-asiatic-research-collection">Rübel Asiatic Research Collection</a> of the Oriental Department was added to the collection</li></ul><ul><li><strong>1979</strong>: The library became one of two documentation centers of the <a href="https://library.harvard.edu/collections/aga-khan-program-islamic-architecture">Aga Khan Program for Islamic Architecture</a>, located at both Harvard and MIT</li><li><strong>1991</strong>: The library expanded into the Werner Otto Hall addition on the east side of the Fogg Museum building</li></ul><figure class="hl__figure--right hl__figure"><img loading="lazy" src="/sites/default/files/styles/content_embed/public/04_Fine%20Arts%20Library%20Reading%20Room%20in%201982%20%20.jpg?itok=AM8-tmMj" width="450" height="299" alt="Fine Arts Library Reading Room in 1982" class="hl__image"><figcaption class="hl__figure__figcaption"><div class="hl__figure__text-caption">
