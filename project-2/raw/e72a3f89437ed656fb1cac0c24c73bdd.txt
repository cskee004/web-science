    <h3>Historic Building Preservation</h3>
    <p>      These historic residential buildings have been redesigned to meet Living Building Challenge Core Certification requirements, a performance-based certification system administered by the International Living Future Institute and PHIUS + 2021 Passive House Certification standards. The project team chose to preserve the original buildings to respect their history and realize significant reductions in embodied carbon through the reuse of existing materials. The buildings utilizes modern technology on the inside while maintaining their historic fabrics externally - responding to the challenge of climate change while honoring their unique backgrounds. 
    </p>

    <h3>5 Sacramento Street</h3>
    <p>      5 Sacramento Street, built in 1891, is valued as a significant Queen Anne Victorian due to its high-quality design and construction. Carrying the legacy of renowned local architect, George Fogerty, this esteemed property respects its history with the preservation of the original structure while prioritizing sustainability and resiliency throughout. It maintains the external historic fabric while internally adapting to the demands of climate change and prioritizing occupant health and well-being through modern technology, rigorous design criteria, and building principles. A first of its kind renovation, it stands as a testament to honoring the past while embracing the future.
    </p>


            </a>

    <h3>13 Kirkland Place</h3>

Its renovation pairs a commitment to historical preservation with a forward-thinking approach to sustainability, climate resilience, and occupant well-being. By skillfully blending rigorous design principles with cutting-edge technology, the property stands as a testament to a rich past and a sustainable future.
    </p>

<section class="paragraph paragraph--type--line-headline-with-text paragraph--view-mode--default bg--beige tpad-def">
  <div class="huh-line-headline__container l-container t-copy">
      <h2 class="huh-line-headline">Building a Sustainable Future</h2>




    
    
    <div class="flex flex-2col flex-space-between">
              <div class="flex-col"><p>Aligned with Harvard’s Sustainability Action Plan and the University’s Goal Zero for a fossil fuel-free Harvard, 5 Sacramento Street and 13 Kirkland Place are both remodeled to meet two ambitious certifications aimed at achieving an all-electric and comprehensively sustainable homes:</p>

<h4 class="simple-heading"><strong>Living Building Challenge Core Certification</strong></h4>

<p>A <a href="https://living-future.org/core/basics/">holistic performance-based certification system</a> administered by the International Living Future Institute (ILFI), outlined by 10 imperatives.&nbsp;After construction, there is a 12-month performance period during which energy and water reduction requirements must be met for LBC Core certification.</p>

<div class="field field--name-field-cta field--type-link field--label-hidden field__item"><a href="https://www.huhousing.harvard.edu/resident-information/sustainability/living#LBC-imperatives">The 10 Imperatives</a></div>
</div>
              <div class="flex-col"><h4 class="simple-heading"><strong>Phius+2021 CORE Certification: Certified October 2023</strong></h4>

<p>A <a href="https://phmass.org/what-is-passive-house/">performance standard from the Passive House Institute US</a> focused on achieving superior building performance and quality construction. <br />
Certification is guided by a set of design principles that emphasize quality, durability, health, safety, and cost-optimized conservation on the path to net-zero energy:</p>

<ol><li>Continuous Insulation</li>
	<li>Airtight Construction</li>
	<li>Optimized Windows</li>
	<li>Balanced Ventilation</li>
	<li>Minimized Mechanical Systems</li>
</ol><p>Throughout the construction of these properties, a Phius Certified Verifier provided 3rd party quality assurance and oversight via on-site inspections and performance testing.</p>

<p> </p></div>
          </div>

  

      
  </div>

            <div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-field-body-copy field--type-text-long field--label-hidden field__item"><h3>Landscapes</h3>

<p>The sites are designed to encourage ecological regeneration and enhance the function of the project sites for the community. Key measures include:</p>

<ul>
	<li>Soil amendment</li>
	<li>On-site rainwater infiltration and stormwater management</li>
	<li>Tree preservation</li>
	<li>Promotion of biodiversity, native species selection, and drought tolerant plantings</li>
	<li>Incorporation of pollinator gardens</li>
	<li>Community gathering spaces</li>
</ul>

<p>The landscaping has been designed to ensure resilience and sustainability in the face of challenging environmental conditions.</p>

<h3>Water</h3>

<p>The buildings treat water like a precious resource, minimizing waste and the use of potable water while avoiding downstream impacts and pollution. A 30% reduction target is accomplished by low-flow fixtures, water-saving appliances and equipment, and low-impact landscape design techniques that eliminate the need for irrigation.</p>

<h3>Materials</h3>

<p>The projects follow <a href="https://sustainable.harvard.edu/organization/harvard-healthier-building-academy/">Harvard University’s Healthier Building Academy</a> (HHBA) product criteria and standards. The HHBA sets material-health specifications aimed at removing chemical classes of concern and identifying healthier products through category-specific requirements - the fundamental requirement being complete product transparency. Material selection prioritized local sourcing and the project’s lumber was either responsibly sourced or salvaged. The project team made strategic use of existing materials in the building, such as siding, sheathing, brick, and concrete floors to reduce embodied carbon.</p>

<p>Combined with the PHIUS building measures, a high-performance energy system with heat pump driven technology was selected to meet ambitious Energy Use Intensity (EUI) targets for both buildings.</p>

<p>Far exceeding the ILFI Core requirement of a 50% energy reduction compared to baseline buildings of the same size and function, 13 Kirkland Place and 5 Sacramento Street minimize energy-related carbon emissions that contribute to climate change.</p>

<h3>Human Health</h3>

<p>Optimizing occupant health drove the majority of design decisions on the project. Several priorities were adopted into the design of these buildings, including:</p>

<ul><li>Providing exceptional indoor air quality and a healthy interior environment for residents</li>
	<li>Creating outdoor spaces that promote cycling and walking</li>
	<li>Ensuring design centered around universal access</li>

      <div class="huh-accordion__intro_row" data-headline="How did we do it?">
  
    <h3>How did we do it?</h3>
    

    <div class="t-copy"> 
    <div class="intro_text"><h4>Learn how these buildings align with the Living Building Challenge through innovative construction &amp; design for a more sustainable future:</h4></div>
  </div>
    
  <div class="t-copy">
    
    <div class="huh-accordion__nested js-nested-accordion-group">
  <div class="huh-accordion__row huh-accordion__row--nested js-nested-accordion" data-headline="Ecology of Place">
  <button aria-controls="accordion-section-ecology-of-place" aria-expanded="false" class="huh-accordion__intro js-nested-accordion-toggle">
    <h4>Ecology of Place</h4>
    <div class="huh-accordion__toggle" aria-hidden="true">
      <span></span>
    </div>
  </button>
  <div id="accordion-section-ecology-of-place" class="huh-accordion__body js-nested-accordion-body t-copy">
    <p>The intent of this Imperative is to protect wild and ecologically significant places and encourage ecological regeneration and enhanced function of the communities and places where projects are built. Several key elements were incorporated to promote ecological improvement:</p>

<ul>
	<li>The subgrade was de-compacted to facilitate the infiltration of water and the flow of oxygen to plant roots.</li>
	<li>New soil was added to provide adequate support for plant growth.</li>
	<li>Localized low points were created to address stormwater runoff, serving the dual purpose of collecting runoff and allowing for increased infiltration.</li>
</ul>

<p>These areas also presented an opportunity to introduce wetland plants, contributing to the site's ecological diversity. The plantings were strategically chosen to attract pollinator species by supporting their habitat and promoting biodiversity. Lastly, native and drought-tolerant plants were selected to ensure resilience and sustainability in the face of challenging environmental conditions.</p>

  </div>
</div>

  <div class="huh-accordion__row huh-accordion__row--nested js-nested-accordion" data-headline="Human-Scaled Living">
  <button aria-controls="accordion-section-human-scaled-living" aria-expanded="false" class="huh-accordion__intro js-nested-accordion-toggle">
    <h4>Human-Scaled Living</h4>
    <div class="huh-accordion__toggle" aria-hidden="true">
      <span></span>
    </div>
  </button>
  <div id="accordion-section-human-scaled-living" class="huh-accordion__body js-nested-accordion-body t-copy">
    <p>The intent of this Imperative is to contribute toward the creation of walkable, pedestrian-oriented communities that reduce the use of fossil fuel vehicles.</p>

<p>To meet this imperative, the project has implemented several strategies, including providing bike storage for occupants and opportunities to participate in <a href="https://www.transportation.harvard.edu/commuterchoice">Harvard's CommuterChoice program</a> - promoting the use of public transportation and human-powered transit. The project is also working on a plan to reduce single-occupancy vehicle trips by advocating for pedestrian-friendly routes and coordinating carpooling efforts. Regular surveys are conducted to assess current fossil fuel-based travel patterns.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <div class="huh-accordion__row huh-accordion__row--nested js-nested-accordion" data-headline="Responsible Water Use">
  <button aria-controls="accordion-section-responsible-water-use" aria-expanded="false" class="huh-accordion__intro js-nested-accordion-toggle">
    <h4>Responsible Water Use</h4>
    <div class="huh-accordion__toggle" aria-hidden="true">
      <span></span>
    </div>
  </button>
  <div id="accordion-section-responsible-water-use" class="huh-accordion__body js-nested-accordion-body t-copy">
    <p>The intent of this Imperative is to encourage projects to treat water like a precious resource, minimizing waste and the use of potable water while avoiding downstream impacts and pollution. The project aimed to achieve a 30% reduction from baseline water consumption by implementing various measures. This is achieved through the use of low flow fixtures, which help conserve water by reducing the amount used by occupants. Additionally, the project implemented a 'no potable water policy' for irrigation, which is possible through the use of native and drought-tolerant plants. To effectively manage stormwater on site, the project incorporated strategies to reduce impervious areas by adopting green stormwater infrastructure and low-impact design techniques wherever feasible.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <div class="huh-accordion__row huh-accordion__row--nested js-nested-accordion" data-headline="Healthy Interior Environment">
  <button aria-controls="accordion-section-healthy-interior-environment" aria-expanded="false" class="huh-accordion__intro js-nested-accordion-toggle">
    <h4>Healthy Interior Environment</h4>
    <div class="huh-accordion__toggle" aria-hidden="true">
      <span></span>
    </div>
  </button>
  <div id="accordion-section-healthy-interior-environment" class="huh-accordion__body js-nested-accordion-body t-copy">
    <p>The intent of this Imperative is to promote good indoor air quality and a healthy interior environment for project occupants. The project provides outside views and daylight access for 100% of regularly occupied spaces. It also complies with ASHRAE 62 ventilation standards and prohibits smoking within 25 feet of any building opening. The project team has developed a comprehensive Healthy Interior Environment plan that encompasses cleaning protocols, HVAC maintenance, training plans, and strategies for improving air quality and preventing particulate matter.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <div class="huh-accordion__row huh-accordion__row--nested js-nested-accordion" data-headline="Energy + Carbon Reduction">
  <button aria-controls="accordion-section-energy-+-carbon-reduction" aria-expanded="false" class="huh-accordion__intro js-nested-accordion-toggle">
    <h4>Energy + Carbon Reduction</h4>
    <div class="huh-accordion__toggle" aria-hidden="true">
      <span></span>
    </div>
  </button>
  <div id="accordion-section-energy-+-carbon-reduction" class="huh-accordion__body js-nested-accordion-body t-copy">
    <p>The intent of this Imperative is to treat energy as a precious resource and minimize energy-related carbon emissions that contribute to climate change. The project is required to achieve a 50% reduction in energy usage compared to a baseline. Additionally, the buildings are designed to meet PHIUS + 2021 Passive House Certification standard, and the design exceeds the LBC Core requirement of a 50% energy reduction compared to a baseline building of the same size and function.</p>

<p>The anticipated Energy Use Intensity (EUI) is 33 for 13 Kirkland Place, and 23 for 5 Sacramento Street. These EUIs represent significant reductions: 76% for 13 Kirkland Place, and 80% for 5 Sacramento Street.</p>

<p>A high-performance energy system was selected to allow the buildings to meet these ambitious design EUIs. The systems include ERV’s, which recover 9-13 times the amount of energy they use to operate, and heat pumps for both space conditioning and water heating. The space conditioning heat pumps installed produce 2-3 units of heating energy output or 4-5 units of cooling energy output for every one unit of electricity input, and the heat pump water heaters produce hot water with electricity at rates 50 % to 100% more efficient than conventional hot water heaters.</p>

<p>Moreover, the buildings also utilize materials with lower-than-average embodied carbon content for interior materials such as carpet, ceiling tile, and gypsum wall board. The selection of these products further improves the buildings life cycle carbon impact.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <div class="huh-accordion__row huh-accordion__row--nested js-nested-accordion" data-headline="Responsible Materials">
  <button aria-controls="accordion-section-responsible-materials" aria-expanded="false" class="huh-accordion__intro js-nested-accordion-toggle">
    <h4>Responsible Materials</h4>
    <div class="huh-accordion__toggle" aria-hidden="true">
      <span></span>
    </div>
  </button>
  <div id="accordion-section-responsible-materials" class="huh-accordion__body js-nested-accordion-body t-copy">
    <p>The intent of this Imperative is to set a baseline for transparency, sustainable extraction, support of local industry, and waste diversion for all projects.</p>

<p>The buildings incorporate Declare label products - Declare is a material ingredient disclosure label designed to promote market transformation and promote the use of healthier materials in buildings. Additionally, 20% of materials used are sourced locally within a 311-mile radius, and 50% of wood used is either FSC-certified or salvaged. The project team also made strategic use of existing materials in the building, such as refurbishing refrigerators and concrete floors, to reduce embodied carbon. Lastly, 80% of renovation waste was diverted from landfills and recycled.</p>

<p>These projects also follow <a href="https://sustainable.harvard.edu/organization/harvard-healthier-building-academy/">Harvard's Healthier Building Academy</a> (HHBA) product criteria and standards. The HHBA sets material-health specifications for 14 interior product categories required in major new construction and renovation projects at Harvard. It aims to eliminate chemical classes of concern and identify healthier products through category-specific requirements, with the fundamental requirement being complete product transparency.</p>
  </div>
</div>

  <div class="huh-accordion__row huh-accordion__row--nested js-nested-accordion" data-headline="Universal Access">
  <button aria-controls="accordion-section-universal-access" aria-expanded="false" class="huh-accordion__intro js-nested-accordion-toggle">
    <h4>Universal Access</h4>
    <div class="huh-accordion__toggle" aria-hidden="true">
      <span></span>
