    Harvard University Disability Resources (UDR) values disability as integral to our rich diversity
  </h2>
        <div
      class="hwp-hero__eyebrow-title hwp-order-1 hwp-hero__eyebrow-line">
      Welcome
    </div>
        <div class="hwp-hero__description hwp-order-3 hwp-text-block">
      
            <div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><p>Our office provides leadership to University efforts to ensure an accessible, inclusive welcoming learning and working environment for individuals with disabilities while complying with federal and state regulations.&nbsp;</p>
<p>UDR serves as a central resource for students, faculty, staff, and visitors on disability-related information, procedures and services for our community. We also provide expertise in the development, implementation, and acquisition for best and promising disability-related University practices.&nbsp;</p>

        <h2 class="hwp-text-block__heading h2">
      UDR offers the following services
    </h2>
            
            <div class="hwp-text-block field field--name-field-hwp-body field--type-text-long field--label-hidden"><div class="tex2jax_process"><h3>Consultation</h3>
<p>UDR is available to consult with community members related to best practices for accessibility and inclusion of persons with disabilities. Consultation may be initiated by a phone call (617.495.1859 or TTY 617.496.0466), or email to <a href="mailto:disabilityresources@harvard.edu">disabilityresources@harvard.edu</a>. Examples of topics community members may wish to consult on:&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>Resources available to students and employees with disabilities&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
<li>Accommodations statements for programs, events, and applications&nbsp;</li>
<li>Responding to student, employee, and/or visitor reasonable accommodation requests&nbsp;&nbsp;</li>
<li>Best practices for running inclusive and accessible programs&nbsp;</li>
<li>When and how to provide captioning and/or audio descriptions for video or online content&nbsp;</li>
<li>How to run accessible in-person, remote, or hybrid events&nbsp;</li>
<li>Assessing physical accessibility of spaces used for programs and activities&nbsp;</li>
<li>Access for service animals in campus spaces&nbsp;</li>
<li>Effective communication with community members who are deaf or hard of hearing&nbsp;</li>
</ul>
<h3>Training</h3>
<p>UDR is happy to provide education in support of increasing accessibility and inclusion of persons with disabilities in our community. UDR will tailor trainings or topical discussions for departments if a group of ten or more persons agree to participate. UDR also offers a general <a href="https://trainingportal.harvard.edu/Saba/Web_spf/NA1PRD0068/app/me/learningeventdetail/cours000000000017749?regId=regdw000000000889522&amp;returnurl=common%2Fsearchresults%2Fdisability%2FALL">Disability Inclusion</a> class through Harvard’s Center for Workplace Development.&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Technology and Equipment</h3>
<p>UDR’s Assistive Technology Library and Lab (ATLL) offers people the ability to try out technology to see if it is helpful to them. The ATLL also loans assistive technology and equipment such as wheelchairs or assistive listening devices, to any member of our community.&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Accommodations</h3>
<p>Harvard community members with questions about reasonable accommodations for themselves or others may contact UDR at <a href="mailto:disabilityresources@harvard.edu">disabilityresources@harvard.edu</a> for consultation.&nbsp;</p>
<p>*Community members may wish to review UDR’s website for general guidance related to accessibility and our <a href="/frequently-asked-questions" data-entity-type="node" data-entity-uuid="80b9b5de-0720-4ea0-9127-d0725c9869b8" data-entity-substitution="canonical" title="Frequently Asked Questions">Frequently Asked Questions</a> page to learn answers to common questions</p>
