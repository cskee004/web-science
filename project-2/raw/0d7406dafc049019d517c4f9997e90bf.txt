      <div class="hl__full-template__page-content hl__full-template__page-content--stacked" id="teach" tabindex="-1"><div class="hl__comp-header" tab-index="-1"><h2 class="hl__comp-heading ">  Teaching with the Archives
</h2></div></div>  
  





<div class="hl__full-template__page-content hl__full-template__page-content--stacked hl__full-template__page-content--sub-row" id="rich_text-9566" tabindex="-1">
        <section class="hl__show-more-text"><div  id="showMoreText9566"><section class="hl__rich-text"><p class="hl__rte-large">We welcome and encourage teaching with our collections and can provide engaging archival experiences for students. Whether in person or virtual, our staff is ready to connect students to our collections.</p><figure class="hl__figure--right hl__figure"><img loading="lazy" src="/sites/default/files/styles/content_embed/public/archives.jpg?itok=Pq-lcfKX" width="450" height="300" alt="Students examine materials in the Archives as part of a class.                 Students examine materials in the Archives as part of a class.

We offer:</p><ul><li>help with finding archival material for your course</li><li>customized course sessions</li><li>research guides tailored to topics and assignments</li><li>individual research consultations with students</li></ul><p>For more information or to schedule a session at the University Archives for your class, please use the <a href="https://classrequest.library.harvard.edu/">A</a><a href="https://classrequest.library.harvard.edu/ ">rchives and Special Collections Class Request Form</a>.</p></section></div></section>  </div>

      <div class="hl__full-template__page-content hl__full-template__page-content--stacked" id="mission-history-authorization" tabindex="-1"><div class="hl__comp-header" tab-index="-1"><h2 class="hl__comp-heading ">  About
</h2></div></div>  
  





<div class="hl__full-template__page-content hl__full-template__page-content--stacked hl__full-template__page-content--sub-row" id="rich_text-17350" tabindex="-1">
        <section class="hl__show-more-text"><div  id="showMoreText17350"><section class="hl__rich-text"><h3>Welcome Message from Virginia Hunt, University Archivist&nbsp;</h3><p>&nbsp;</p><figure class="hl__figure--left hl__figure"><img loading="lazy" src="/sites/default/files/styles/content_embed/public/Hunt_Ginny_cropped_3.jpg?itok=qZtytfgo" width="450" height="469" alt="Virginia Hunt, University Archivist" class="hl__image"></figure><p>Welcome to the Harvard University Archives!&nbsp; </p><p>Containing 400 years of Harvard, American, and world history, our collections are open to the public and accessible through visits to our Reading Room and exhibition gallery in Pusey Library—both situated right in Harvard Yard—as well as via the constellation of Harvard Library access and discovery portals.</p><p>As the primary repository for Harvard’s records and personal archives, you will find over five centuries of innovation, exploration, revolution, and introspection—the soul of Harvard—on campus and worldwide.&nbsp; A product of careful stewardship, community engagement, and interconnectedness, our vast and inclusive collections provide opportunities for remote and hands-on teaching and learning, public and educational programs, individual and scholarly pursuit, and opportunities for interaction between the creators and users of the University Archives.&nbsp; </p><p>We invite you to come and experience the people, discoveries, change, and history that make up the Harvard University Archives.</p></section></div></section>  </div>

  





<div class="hl__full-template__page-content hl__full-template__page-content--stacked hl__full-template__page-content--sub-row" id="rich_text-17592" tabindex="-1">
        <section class="hl__show-more-text"><div  id="showMoreText17592"><section class="hl__rich-text"><h3>Accessibility of the Harvard University Archives</h3><ul><li>The closest accessible entrance to the Harvard Map Collection and Pusey Library is the main entrance to Lamont Library. This entrance can be accessed via Quincy Street and is accessible by automatic door and ramp. There are two elevators that go down to level B of Lamont that connects to Level 1 of Pusey Library and the Harvard University Archives.</li><li>Lamont Library is not open to the public. If you don’t have a Harvard ID please let us know you are coming and will be entering through Lamont so we can alert the guards.</li></ul><p>Contact Hannah Hack, <a href="mailto:hannah_hack@harvard.edu">hannah_hack@harvard.edu</a>, with questions about accessibility of elevators, study spaces, meeting rooms, and restrooms.</p><h3>Mission</h3><p>The Harvard University Archives, a unit of the Harvard Library, has authorization derived&nbsp;from votes of the Harvard Corporation. The Archives&nbsp;supports the University's mission of education and research by preserving and providing access, to the greatest extent possible, to Harvard's historical records; gathering an accurate, authentic, and complete record of the life of the University; and promoting the highest standards of management for Harvard's current records.</p><h3>History</h3><p>Authorized by a vote of the President and Fellows of Harvard College in 1851, the Harvard University Archives is believed to be the oldest institutional archives in the United States established by a corporation.</p><p>The mission and the authorization for the work of the Harvard University Archives derive from a succession of <a href="https://library.harvard.edu/collections/votes-harvard-corporation">votes by the Harvard Corporation</a>.&nbsp;</p></section></div></section>  </div>
